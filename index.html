Auto-formed groups by course

The greedy max-min algorithm forms cohesive groups: every member must score ≥ 40 with every other member — no weak links.

How the matching works

Every pair of students in the same course gets a compatibility score from 0–100:

  • 40% Availability overlap — shared slots on a 7-day × 4-block grid (saturates at 4 shared slots)
  • 30% Topic overlap — Jaccard similarity of the topics you each listed
  • 15% Study-style affinity — same style is ideal; discussion + practice pairs well, discussion + quiet co-study doesn't
  • 15% Goal alignment — "ace the course" and "just need homework help" is the weakest pairing

Hard vetoes: different course, or zero shared availability → score 0. A group you can never meet with isn't a group.

Group formation

Groups are built greedily with a max-min rule: seed with the best remaining pair, then repeatedly add the student whose minimum score against all current members is highest (and ≥ 40), until the group reaches the smallest preferred size among its members. This keeps groups cohesive — nobody is a bad fit for anybody.

This is a client-side demo: the 16-student cohort is seeded sample data, and profiles you create are stored only in your browser (localStorage). The full engine is unit-tested — see the GitHub repo.
